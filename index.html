NBG Onboarding Quiz

Question 1 / 22

What is the primary purpose of the Standard Specifications Guide?

Feedback:

The guide's main purpose is to "provide the owner or design professional a detailed description of all standard NBG products" and "aid the architect or engineer of record in the process of specifying the best building system solution."

Question 2 / 22

Which manual is incorporated by reference into all NBG project specifications?

Feedback:

Correct. The guide states it "incorporates, by reference, the latest edition of the MBMA Metal Building Systems Manual... to all NBG project specifications." While other codes (like AISC and IBC) are used in design, the MBMA manual is the one incorporated by reference for overall practices.

Question 3 / 22

True or False: NBG will provide items that are not specifically called for in the NBG order documents.

Feedback:

This is False. The guide is very clear: "NBG will not provide items not specifically called for in the NBG order documents."

Question 4 / 22

How is the building "eave height" defined?

Feedback:

Eave height is specifically defined as "the distance from finished floor to top of eave strut or purlin at the steel line."

Question 5 / 22

How is the building "width" defined?

Feedback:

Building width is measured "from outside face of sidewall girt to outside face of sidewall girt (steel line to steel line)."

Question 6 / 22

Who is considered the "Engineer of Record" (EOR) for the project?

Feedback:

This is a key concept. The guide explicitly states, "NBG's sealing engineer is not the 'Engineer of Record' for the project and cannot be construed as such." The EOR is the design professional working for the owner.

Question 7 / 22

Does NBG supply the anchor bolts for the foundation?

Feedback:

Correct. "Anchor bolts shall not be supplied by NBG and are the responsibility of the contractor or erector to furnish."

Question 8 / 22

Who is responsible for designing the building's foundations?

Feedback:

NBG does not supply the foundation design. The guide states, "Foundations shall be designed by a qualified registered professional engineer to accommodate the local soil conditions" and "Neither the foundation design nor the reinforcing steel shall be supplied by NBG."

Question 9 / 22

How many sets of final erection drawings does NBG supply with all orders?

Feedback:

The guide specifies, "NBG shall supply on all orders three sets of final erection drawings."

Question 10 / 22

What is the difference between "primary" and "secondary" structural members?

Feedback:

This is the correct definition. Primary members (like columns/frames) "transfer their loads directly to the structure's foundation." Secondary members (like purlins/girts) "transfer their loads from the cladding to the primary structural member."

Question 11 / 22

Purlins and girts are examples of which type of structural member?

Feedback:

Correct. Purlins (for the roof) and girts (for the walls) are defined as "secondary structural" members. They support the panels and transfer the load to the primary framing.

Question 12 / 22

What color is the standard shop primer applied to structural members that are not galvanized?

Feedback:

The standard is "one shop coat of a red or gray primer."

Question 13 / 22

What is the *minimum* recommended roof slope for the NBG R-Panel Roof Panel?

Feedback:

Correct. The "Minimum recommended roof slope for NBG R-Panel is 1/2" in 12"." The 1/4" in 12" option is tricky because that is the minimum for *other* roofs (like standing seam).

Question 14 / 22

What is the *minimum* roof slope for the NBG Trapezoidal Standing Seam Roof Systems (like CFR, SS360, and SSII)?

Feedback:

Correct. The "Minimum roof slope is 1/4" in 12"" for these standing seam systems. This is a lower slope than the R-Panel, which requires 1/2" in 12".

Question 15 / 22

What is the *minimum* roof slope for the NBG Insulated CFR Metal Panel Roof System?

Feedback:

Correct. The "Minimum roof slope is 1/2" in 12"" for the Insulated CFR panel, the same as the R-Panel.

Question 16 / 22

For the NBG Insulated CFR Metal Panel Roof System, what is the required erection direction?

Feedback:

The guide states these panels "will be installed from left to right since they are not symmetrical."

Question 17 / 22

What kind of tools *must* be used for field-cutting standing seam roof panels to avoid voiding the warranty?

Feedback:

Correct. You must use "non-abrasive cutting tools such as nibblers or tin-snips." Using abrasive tools "can damage the GalvalumeÂ® or painted finish... and may void your manufacturer's warranty."

Question 18 / 22

What should be done daily during roof installation to prevent damage from oxidation?

Feedback:

The roof surface "should be cleaned daily during construction of all filings, cuttings, screws, pencil markings, and debris to prevent damage due to oxidation of foreign materials."

Question 19 / 22

Which of the following is NOT one of the standard (non-insulated) wall covering profiles listed in Section 10?

Feedback:

This is a tricky one! Section 10 lists "R-Panel, Reverse R-Panel or A-Panel profile." Loc Seam (Section 8) is a *roof* covering system, not a standard wall panel from Section 10.

Question 20 / 22

How are fasteners typically handled for Insulated Metal Wall Panels?

Feedback:

Correct. The guide notes, "The panel fasteners are concealed within the panel lap" and "Sidelap panel clips and fasteners are concealed within the lap of the panels."

Question 21 / 22

What is the main benefit of "Prismatic Skylights"?

Feedback:

The key benefit is that the prisms "refract sunlight into micro light beams, spreading the sun's bright natural light... no direct sunlight-therefore no damaging hot spots, no glares..." They provide "100% diffusion."

Question 22 / 22

True or False: Translucent light panels are designed to support foot traffic.

Feedback:

This is False. The guide explicitly states, "The buyer shall advise all parties and the owner that translucent panels are not designed for foot traffic."
