NBG Specifications Onboarding Quiz

Question 1 / 22

What is the primary purpose of the Standard Specifications Guide?

Answer:

To provide the owner or design professional with a detailed description of all standard NBG products and to help the architect or engineer of record specify the best building system solution.

Key Point to Remember:

The guide's main purpose is to describe all standard NBG products and assist the architect or engineer in specifying the best solution.

Question 2 / 22

Which manual, published by the MBMA, is incorporated by reference into all NBG project specifications?

Answer:

The MBMA Metal Building Systems Manual.

Key Point to Remember:

The correct answer is the MBMA Metal Building Systems Manual. This manual is referenced throughout the guide.

Question 3 / 22

True or False: NBG will provide items that are not specifically called for in the NBG order documents.

Answer:

False.

Key Point to Remember:

This is false. The guide is very clear: "NBG will not provide items not specifically called for in the NBG order documents".

Question 4 / 22

How is the building "eave height" defined?

Answer:

The distance from the finished floor to the top of the eave strut or purlin at the steel line.

Key Point to Remember:

Eave height is measured from the finished floor to the top of the eave strut or purlin at the steel line.

Question 5 / 22

How is the building "width" defined?

Answer:

The distance from the outside face of one sidewall girt to the outside face of the opposing sidewall girt (steel line to steel line).

Key Point to Remember:

Building width isn't the total building footprint. It's specifically measured from the outside face of the sidewall girt to the outside face of the opposite sidewall girt.

Question 6 / 22

True or False: The NBG sealing engineer is the "Engineer of Record" for the project.

Answer:

False.

Key Point to Remember:

This is false. The guide states, "NBG's sealing engineer is not the 'Engineer of Record' for the project and cannot be construed as such".

Question 7 / 22

Does NBG supply the anchor bolts for the foundation?

Answer:

No.

Key Point to Remember:

NBG does not supply anchor bolts. They are the "responsibility of the contractor or erector to furnish".

Question 8 / 22

Who is responsible for designing the building's foundations?

Answer:

A qualified registered professional engineer (retained by the customer/contractor). NBG does not supply the foundation design.

Key Point to Remember:

NBG does not design the foundation. It must be designed by a qualified registered professional engineer to handle local soil conditions and building reactions.

Question 9 / 22

How many sets of final erection drawings does NBG supply with all orders?

Answer:

Three sets.

Key Point to Remember:

NBG supplies three sets of final erection drawings on all orders.

Question 10 / 22

What is the difference between "primary" and "secondary" structural members?

Answer:

Primary members (like columns or frames) transfer loads directly to the foundation. Secondary members (like purlins or girts) transfer loads from the cladding (walls/roof) to the primary members.

Key Point to Remember:

Remember it this way: Primary members touch the foundation. Secondary members (like purlins and girts) transfer loads *from* the roof and wall panels *to* the primary members.

Question 11 / 22

Purlins and girts are examples of which type of structural member (Primary or Secondary)?

Answer:

Secondary structural members.

Key Point to Remember:

They are secondary members. They are roll-formed "Z" or "C" sections that support the roof (purlins) or wall (girts) panels and transfer the load to the primary framing.

Question 12 / 22

What color is the standard shop primer applied to structural members that are not galvanized?

Answer:

One shop coat of red or gray primer.

Key Point to Remember:

The standard primer is either red or gray.

Question 13 / 22

What is the *minimum* recommended roof slope for the NBG R-Panel Roof Panel?

Answer:

1/2" in 12".

Key Point to Remember:

The minimum slope for an R-Panel roof is 1/2" in 12".

Question 14 / 22

What is the *minimum* roof slope for the NBG Trapezoidal Standing Seam Roof Systems (like CFR, SS360, and SSII)?

Answer:

1/4" in 12".

Key Point to Remember:

The minimum slope for these standing seam systems is 1/4" in 12", which is a lower slope than the R-Panel.

Question 15 / 22

What is the *minimum* roof slope for the NBG Insulated CFR Metal Panel Roof System?

Answer:

1/2" in 12".

Key Point to Remember:

The minimum slope for the Insulated CFR roof is 1/2" in 12".

Question 16 / 22

For the NBG Insulated CFR Metal Panel Roof System, what is the required erection direction?

Answer:

From left to right.

Key Point to Remember:

These panels must be erected from left to right because they are not symmetrical.

Question 17 / 22

What kind of tools *must* be used for field-cutting standing seam roof panels to avoid voiding the warranty?

Answer:

Non-abrasive cutting tools, such as nibblers or tin-snips.

Key Point to Remember:

You must use non-abrasive tools like nibblers or tin-snips. Abrasive tools (like grinders or saws) can damage the finish, create metal shavings that corrode the panel, and may void the warranty.

Question 18 / 22

What should be done daily during roof installation to prevent damage from oxidation?

Answer:

The roof surface should be cleaned of all filings, cuttings, screws, pencil markings, and debris.

Key Point to Remember:

The roof must be cleaned daily of all debris, especially metal filings and cuttings, which can rust and cause damage to the panels.

Question 19 / 22

Name two of the three standard (non-insulated) wall covering profiles mentioned in Section 10.

Answer:

Any two of the following: R-Panel, Reverse R-Panel, or A-Panel.

Key Point to Remember:

The three standard wall profiles listed are the R-Panel, Reverse R-Panel, and A-Panel.

Question 20 / 22

Are the fasteners for Insulated Metal Wall Panels typically concealed or exposed?

Answer:

Concealed.

Key Point to Remember:

The fasteners are concealed within the panel lap or by sidelap panel clips.

Question 21 / 22

What is the main benefit of "Prismatic Skylights" according to the guide?

Answer:

They refract sunlight into micro-beams, spreading natural light without "hot spots," glares, or UV damage. They also transmit 35% more light than standard industrial skylights.

Key Point to Remember:

The key benefit is that their prisms diffuse light 100%, which prevents damaging hot spots and glare while transmitting 35% more light.

Question 22 / 22

True or False: Translucent light panels are designed to support foot traffic.

Answer:

False.

Key Point to Remember:

This is false. The guide explicitly states, "The buyer shall advise all parties and the owner that translucent panels are not designed for foot traffic".
